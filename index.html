The Fall of Adam and Eve

The impact on your eternal family

Adam and Eve

"God prepared this earth as a home for His children. Adam and Eve were chosen to be the first people to live on the earth. Their part in our Father’s plan was to bring mortality into the world. They were to be the first parents."

"And the first man of all men have I called Adam, which is many" Moses 1:34

"And Adam called his wife's name Eve, because she was the mother of all living; for thus have I, the Lord God, called the first of all women, which are many."                                                                 Moses 4:26

Why Adam?

In the spirit world Adam was called Michael the archangel. He was chosen by our Heavenly Father to lead the righteous in the battle against Satan. The Lord promised Adam great blessings: “I have set thee to be at the head; a multitude of nations shall come of thee, and thou art a prince over them forever”.

Why Eve?

Eve was “the mother of all living”. God brought Adam and Eve together in marriage because “it was not good that the man should be alone”. She shared Adam’s responsibility and will also share his eternal blessings.

Their Companionship

"And the Lord God said, It is not good that the man should be alone; I will make him an help meet for him."                                                                          Genesis 2:18

As Eve was created and introduced to Adam, she was to be "an helpmeet". This means that she was to be an equal partner with Adam in the garden. They were to tend the garden and have joy together. They were also supposed to make decisions together, which they did.

Your Companionship

"Husband and wife have a solemn responsibility to love and care for each other and for their children."

"...fathers and mothers are obligated to help one another as equal partners."

Just as Adam and Eve were required to live in an equal marriage relationship, husbands and wives sealed according to God's law are also under the same obligation to love and care for each other in an equal, eternal marriage relationship sealed by the Holy Spirit of Promise.

Testimony

I am grateful to know that God has a plan for each and every one of us. That plan began long ago and was carefully constructed so as to enable all of God's children to receive eternal life. Beginning wtih Adam and Eve, God has shown the way and provided examples, even a perfect example in Jesus Christ. I know that because of Christ, we do not have to suffer for Adam's transgression or even our own transgressions. I know that the gospel of Jesus Christ applies to all families, big or small, united or not. Christ cares for each individual and family so much that He came to earth and died for them. His sacrifice for our imperfections is perfect, and I know that with our effort to repent, that Atonement can make us clean and whole again. We will live with our families forever in pure joy as long as we live the gospel and keep the commandments of God. I love the gospel of Jesus Christ and am so grateful for it in my life.
